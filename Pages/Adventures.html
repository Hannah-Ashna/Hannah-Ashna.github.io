HackNotts Weekend:

16 - 17 Nov 2019

For the weekend I attended the HackNotts 2019 event. As a newbie to the world of hacking, I found it to be quite an insightful experience as I had the opportunity to learn more about it with the help of mentors present at the venue.

We even had the chance to work on our little personal project during the event which helped hone our python skills for our upcoming test. We created a program that is based on the Hideo Kojima Name Generator worksheet. We decided to automate most of the logic behind the worksheet and hence came up with our own little version of the name generator. It was a whole lot of fun watching people from DevSoc try it out and end up with peculiar names. My favourite one so far is “The Menacing Migraine”.

I'm definitely looking forward to spending a lot of my weekends at upcoming hackathons in the future!

Digital Horizons Week:

11 - 14 Nov 2019

For Nottingham Trent University's Digital Horizons Week, I attended a workshop session with Dr Aygul Zagidullina, a Google Developer Expert for Assistant. It was an incredibly insightful and exciting experience as we got to fiddle around with making our own 'action' (google assistant application).

The next day, we participated in the Technical Challenge. We were asked to create an action that would display information regarding all of the student services provided by NTU when asked. As soon as it began we quickly logged on to create our action and start planning out its structure. It was a lot of fun to work on. Our group won the challenge that day and we couldn’t have been more proud of our little creation; especially since we had only learnt about how to make it not more than 24hours prior.
